# LinkedIn Post Formats — Agent Reference

This file defines the 10 post formats available. Use these rules when writing LinkedIn posts. Each format has a structure, rules, and a word budget. All posts stay under 2800 characters.

---

## Format 1: Steps

**When to use:** Topic has a clear process or how-to. Good for actionable advice.

**Structure:**
- Hook: 9 words or fewer. Creates curiosity or promises a result.
- Problem/context: Why this matters. 30-50 words.
- Numbered list: 3-7 steps. Each step = one specific action + brief explanation.
- Closing: What happens when they follow the steps. CTA optional.

**Rules:**
- Each step starts with a verb ("Do X", "Ask Y", "Write Z")
- No padding between steps
- Steps must be genuinely sequential, not just a list of tips
- Include at least one timeframe or metric to add credibility

**Example structure:**
```
[Hook — 9 words]

[Why this matters — 2-3 sentences]

1. [Action verb + specific instruction]
2. [Action verb + specific instruction]
3. [Action verb + specific instruction]

[What changes when they do this]
```

## Format 2: Stats

**When to use:** Topic has surprising numbers. Builds authority. Good for futurism bucket.

**Structure:**
- Lead with the most counterintuitive stat
- Explain why the stat matters
- 2-3 supporting stats or observations
- Personal take or implication
- Question or CTA

**Rules:**
- Never fabricate stats. If writing from user's niche, ask them to provide the numbers.
- Stat must be in the first line or first two lines
- Explain what the stat means, don't just list it
- End with opinion, not another stat

---

## Format 3: Mistakes

**When to use:** Audience makes predictable errors. Builds authority by naming what others miss.

**Structure:**
- Hook: A mistake most people make (stated as fact)
- Why the mistake is so common
- 3-5 numbered mistakes
- What to do instead (brief, not a full how-to)
- CTA or closing insight

**Rules:**
- Each mistake must be specific, not vague ("posting inconsistently" vs. "not having a content strategy")
- Include a "why it happens" for each mistake
- Don't lecture. State the mistake, explain why it's wrong, move on.

---

## Format 4: Lessons

**When to use:** Personal experience or observation. "What I learned from X."

**Structure:**
- Hook: The moment or situation (moment in time opener works well)
- What happened / the experience
- 3-5 numbered lessons extracted from it
- Why this matters now
- Question to audience

**Rules:**
- Must feel personal. First-person throughout.
- Lessons should be non-obvious. Don't state what anyone would already know.
- Each lesson gets 1-3 sentences max
- Include at least one vulnerable or honest moment

---

## Format 5: Examples

**When to use:** Making an abstract concept concrete. "Here's what this looks like in practice."

**Structure:**
- Hook: State the principle or insight
- 3-5 real-world examples that prove it
- Brief explanation of why each example works
- Broader implication
- CTA or question

**Rules:**
- Examples must be specific (real companies, real behaviors, not "imagine a company that...")
- Vary the examples across industries or contexts
- Each example should surprise or teach something
- End with synthesis, not just another example

---

## Format 6: Story (Narrative)

**When to use:** Building trust and emotional connection. Personal transformation or "I tried this" posts.

**Structure:**
- Hook: 9 words or fewer. A moment in time or vulnerable statement.
- Setup/Problem: 30-50 words. What was happening.
- Story/Example: 80-120 words. The experience, what you tried, what went wrong or right.
- Key Insight: 20-30 words. The one thing you took away.
- Actionable Tips: 40-60 words. What readers can apply.
- CTA: 10-20 words. Genuine question about reader's experience.

**Rules:**
- First 125 characters must end with a curiosity gap (not a complete thought)
- Use specific timeframes, numbers, and names
- Write conversationally — short punchy sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones
- Include one "aha moment" that feels genuinely insightful
- Do NOT ask "What do you think?" as the final question. Make it specific.

**Engagement triggers (must include):**
- Specific timeframe
- Exact metric or number
- Contrarian insight or surprising revelation
- Direct question to audience
- Clear next step

## Format 7: Case Study

**When to use:** Breaking down a real example from business. Analytical, expertise-building content.

**Hook formula:** [Timeframe] + [Specific Person/Company] + [Unexpected Outcome/Action]

**Opening styles:**
- "9 months ago, [Person] did [specific thing]. The results might surprise you:"
- "[Timeframe] ago, [specific example]. Here's the breakdown:"
- "This [person/company] went [specific result]. So let's dig into [what they did]:"

**Structure:**
1. Hook with specific outcome (under 15 words)
2. Brief context — why this matters
3. Transition: "Here's how it worked:" / "A few takeaways:" / "The breakdown:"
4. 3-5 numbered insights with specific details and metrics
5. Personal connection or broader implication
6. Genuine question for discussion

**Tone:** Conversational but analytical. Specific details over vague generalizations. Personal opinions mixed with factual observations.

**Rules:**
- No brackets or placeholder language in the final post
- Every insight includes: what they did, why it worked, the metric that proves it
- Closing question asks about reader's experience with similar approaches
- 200-300 words

---

## Format 8: Hot Take

**When to use:** Challenging conventional wisdom. Opinion-driven. High engagement, some controversy.

**Template structure:**
```
"[Popular belief stated as a quote — 9 words or less]"

[Hook hinting at your expertise/experience with this issue]

Here's the problem:

[State common belief, then counter with evidence]

[Build on previous point]

[Give the strongest argument]

[2-3 sentences of direct experience that supports your view]

- [Alternative action 1]
- [Alternative action 2]
- [Alternative action 3]

[Final insight that reframes the original belief constructively]

[Engagement question]
```

**Rules:**
- Open with the common belief in quotes — makes it feel like you're responding to someone
- Don't be preachy. State your case and move on.
- The 3 alternative actions must be concrete, not more opinions
- End by reframing, not by just attacking the belief

## Format 9: X vs Y (Comparison)

**When to use:** Two approaches, strategies, or mindsets to contrast. Take a clear stance.

**Opening hooks:**
- "Most [people] think [X]. Successful ones do [Y]."
- "[X] vs [Y]. The difference is everything."
- "I've tried both [X] and [Y]. Here's what works:"
- "Everyone's obsessed with [X]. But [Y] is what actually matters."

**Structure:**
1. Hook contrasting the two approaches (under 10 words)
2. Brief context on why this comparison matters
3. Describe X with specific traits/examples
4. Describe Y with contrasting traits/examples
5. Personal story or specific example showing the difference (2-3 sentences)
6. Key insight: why one works better
7. What the better approach actually requires
8. Practical takeaway
9. Engaging question

**Rules:**
- Weave contrasts into paragraphs, not bullet-pointed lists of traits
- Take a clear stance. No "both have merit" cop-outs.
- Use specific examples, not abstractions
- 200-300 words

---

## Format 10: Viral Drop

**When to use:** Short, punchy insight that stands alone. High shareability. Good for engagement spikes.

**Core principle:** One idea, fully expressed, no filler. Every sentence earns its place.

**Structure options:**
- Single insight + 3 supporting points + CTA
- Contrarian take + evidence + reframe
- Numbered list of 5-7 tight observations on one topic

**Rules:**
- Under 150 words
- No setup or preamble — open with the sharpest line
- Each sentence must add new information or angle
- No generic closers ("Hope this helps", "Let me know your thoughts")
- If ending with a question, make it specific to the content

---

## Universal Rules (All Formats)

- Max 2800 characters
- Line breaks every 2-3 sentences
- No emojis unless the user's voice template uses them
- Symbols (→ • ↓) for visual hierarchy are fine
- Write in the user's voice — check `training/voice/voice-template.md` first
- Save output to `output/` with filename `[date]-[topic-slug].md`
