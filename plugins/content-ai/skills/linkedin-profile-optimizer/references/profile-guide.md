# LinkedIn Profile Guide — Agent Reference

This guide defines frameworks for every major profile section. Use these when optimizing any part of a user's LinkedIn profile.

---

## About Section — 7 Frameworks

The About section is 250-300 words. Write like you're explaining your work to someone at a barbecue, not pitching them. Use real moments, actual thoughts, honest observations. Sentences should flow naturally — some short, some long.

### Framework 1: Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)
1. **Problem**: Open with a clear pain point your audience feels
2. **Agitate**: Show negative outcomes, FOMO, costs of inaction
3. **Solution**: Offer your approach, with social proof and CTA

Best for: Service providers, consultants, coaches. Works when the reader has a defined problem you solve.

### Framework 2: Achievement-Realisation-Results (ARR)
1. **Achievement**: Lead with an impressive credential or authority marker
2. **Realisation**: The pivot or insight that shaped your current approach
3. **Results**: What you now create for others
4. End with proof + CTA

Best for: Established experts with track record. Lead with your biggest win, then explain the journey.

### Framework 3: Story-Driven Relatability
1. Open with a conversational backstory or common struggle
2. Align with the reader: "I've been there too"
3. Transition to how you now help others overcome the same issue
4. Drop in accomplishments/social proof
5. Warm, human CTA

Best for: Coaches and educators. Building trust before authority.

### Framework 4: Contrarian Insight
1. Open with an ideal scenario or common assumption
2. Flip it: show why the assumption fails
3. Present your fresh perspective or process
4. Support with proof/results
5. End with alignment + CTA

Best for: People with a non-obvious point of view on their industry.

### Framework 5: WHO → WHAT → HOW
1. State WHO you help (be specific)
2. Clarify WHAT you help them achieve (concrete outcome)
3. Explain HOW (process, philosophy, service)
4. Back with benefits/social proof
5. CTA with urgency or exclusivity

Best for: Freelancers and consultants who need clarity fast. Works well for niche specialists.

### Framework 6: Future Pacing / Vision Casting
1. Paint a desirable future for the reader
2. Show the gap between now and that future
3. Introduce your method as the bridge
4. Reinforce with social proof
5. CTA inviting them into that future

Best for: Transformation-oriented services. Coaching, training, education.

### Framework 7: Authority-First Showcase
1. Open by establishing authority: credentials or big wins
2. Layer in how that experience led to unique insight
3. Translate authority into tangible benefits for clients
4. End with CTA + ways to connect

Best for: People with genuinely impressive credentials who aren't using them well.

## Headline

The headline is the most-seen element on LinkedIn. It appears everywhere: search results, comments, connection requests.

**Formula options:**

1. **Who + What + Result**: "LinkedIn Consultant | Helping B2B founders get 50+ qualified leads/month"
2. **Problem → Solution**: "Struggling with LinkedIn? I turn profiles into pipelines"
3. **Niche + Method + Outcome**: "Email Copywriter → Turning newsletters into $10k/mo revenue"
4. **Credibility + Focus**: "Ex-Google PM | Now helping startups build products people actually want"

**Rules:**
- Under 220 characters
- Front-load the most important information (it gets cut on mobile)
- Avoid generic titles like "Entrepreneur" or "Growth Hacker"
- Include at least one specific, concrete claim
- Do NOT use the word "passionate"

## Banner Text

The banner is visual real estate above your profile photo. If it has text, that text must work alone.

**What to put on a banner:**
- Your positioning statement (who you help + what outcome)
- A strong CTA with URL or handle
- Your core credential + niche

**Banner text rules:**
- Under 10 words (must be readable at thumbnail size)
- One clear message — not a list
- Match the energy of the About section

**Examples:**
- "Helping SaaS founders close $50k+ deals on LinkedIn"
- "Write once. Reach 10,000. Every week."
- "AI coaching for freelancers who want to scale without hiring"

## Skills Section

LinkedIn allows 50 skills. The top 3 appear without "See more." Those top 3 are the ones that matter.

**How to choose top 3 skills:**
1. What do your best clients hire you for? Lead with that.
2. What skill is most searchable in your niche?
3. What skill differentiates you from competitors?

**Rules:**
- Top 3 must be specific, not generic ("LinkedIn Lead Generation" beats "Sales")
- Avoid filler skills that don't add credibility (everyone has "Microsoft Office")
- Align skills with keywords clients search for
- Skills with endorsements rank higher in search — prioritize endorsable skills

## Positioning Bio (Short-Form)

Used in introductions, event bios, speaker pages. 50-75 words.

**Structure:**
- Line 1: Who you help and what outcome you create (one sentence)
- Line 2: How you do it / your unique approach (one sentence)
- Line 3: Credibility or proof point (one sentence)
- Line 4: CTA or where to learn more

**Tone:** Third person or first person, depending on context. Never write in first person for third-party bios.

---

## Profile Optimization Checklist

Before finalizing any profile section, verify:

- [ ] Headline includes specific niche and outcome
- [ ] About section uses one of the 7 frameworks
- [ ] About section is 250-300 words
- [ ] About section ends with a CTA
- [ ] Skills top 3 match what clients hire them for
- [ ] Banner text is under 10 words and readable
- [ ] No generic phrases: "passionate about", "results-driven", "thought leader"
- [ ] At least one specific metric or achievement included

---

## Input Required from User

Before writing any profile section, ask the user for:

1. **Role/Industry**: What do you do and in what space?
2. **Target Audience**: Who specifically do you want to attract?
3. **Main Problem You Solve**: What's the one thing clients come to you for?
4. **Key Results/Achievements**: Any metrics, wins, or credentials?
5. **Unique Method/Approach**: What makes you different from others in your space?
6. **Preferred Contact Method**: How should people reach you? (DM, email, call booking link)

Generate multiple framework versions when appropriate and let the user pick.
