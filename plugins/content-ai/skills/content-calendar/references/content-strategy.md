# Content Strategy Reference — Agent Reference

This file defines the frameworks for content planning: pillars, buckets, audience archetypes, and the Magical Multipliers system. Use these when generating content calendars or brainstorming ideas.

---

## The 5 Variables of a Content System

A complete content system has five variables. When generating a calendar, you need at least Variables 1-3.

### Variable 1: Content Pillars

Content Pillars are the 3-5 essential knowledge areas your audience must master to achieve their desired outcome. They form the foundation of all content.

**How to define pillars:**
Ask: "What are the 3-5 things my audience absolutely must understand or do to succeed?"

Each pillar should be:
- Broad enough to explore from multiple angles for months
- Focused enough that the audience immediately sees relevance
- Aligned with the creator's expertise

**Test:** Is the pillar too generic ("business") or too specific ("Instagram carousel design for dentists")? Both are wrong. Find the middle.

**Example (Real Estate Investing niche):**
1. Property Acquisition (finding deals, market analysis, due diligence)
2. Financing Strategies (loans, partnerships, creative financing)
3. Property Management (tenant screening, cash flow optimization, scaling)

### Variable 2: Content Buckets

The 3 universal content types that work on professional networks. Every piece of content lives in one bucket:

**Bucket 1 — Futurism**
Where your industry/niche is headed. Trends, predictions, changes, market shifts.
- Establishes expertise and forward-thinking
- Good for: industry analysis, "what's coming" posts, trend breakdowns

**Bucket 2 — Your Story**
Lessons learned from personal experience in the niche. Relevant to the reader.
- Builds trust through vulnerability and experience
- Good for: failures, breakthroughs, behind-the-scenes, client transformations you facilitated

**Bucket 3 — Actionable Advice**
How to do something specific. Immediate value.
- Demonstrates competence
- Good for: step-by-step guides, frameworks, checklists, tool recommendations

**How to use buckets:**
Take any pillar and run it through each bucket to get 3 distinct post ideas.

Example — "Property Acquisition" pillar:
- Futurism: "The real estate market is shifting toward these 3 property types in 2025"
- Your Story: "I lost $15K on my second property because I skipped this one step"
- Actionable Advice: "5 websites to analyze any rental property in under 10 minutes"

### Variable 3: Magical Multipliers

The 10 angles that transform any single topic into multiple distinct content pieces.

| Multiplier | What it produces |
|------------|-----------------|
| Tips | Actionable advice, hacks, shortcuts, best practices |
| Stats | Data-driven insights, research findings, survey results, industry numbers |
| Steps | Process breakdowns, tutorials, how-to guides, workflows |
| Lessons | Learning from experience, wisdom gained, insights discovered |
| Benefits | Advantages, positive outcomes, value propositions, ROI cases |
| Reasons | Arguments, explanations, rationale, justifications |
| Mistakes | Common errors, pitfalls, what not to do, warning signs |
| Examples | Real-world cases, specific instances, illustrations |
| Questions | FAQs, prompts to ask yourself, interview questions |
| Personal Stories | First-person narratives, transformations, specific moments |

**How to use:** Take any topic + any multiplier = a unique post angle.

Example — Topic: "LinkedIn content strategy"
- Tips for LinkedIn content strategy
- Stats that prove why LinkedIn content works
- Steps to build a LinkedIn content strategy from scratch
- Lessons I learned building my LinkedIn content strategy
- Mistakes people make with LinkedIn content strategy
- Examples of LinkedIn content strategies that worked

Each angle creates genuinely different content that serves different reader needs.

### Variable 4: Audience Archetypes

The distinct segments within your niche. Tailor the same core message for maximum relevance to each segment.

**The 5 segmentation dimensions:**

| Dimension | Examples |
|-----------|---------|
| Experience level | Beginners vs. experienced practitioners vs. transitioning experts |
| Role/position | Individual contributors vs. managers vs. C-suite |
| Industry/vertical | Tech vs. healthcare vs. manufacturing vs. professional services |
| Life stage | Students vs. early career vs. mid-career vs. late career |
| Business size | Solopreneurs vs. small business vs. enterprise vs. startups |

**Rule:** Each archetype must be specific enough that a person in that segment thinks "this was written for me." Generic archetypes ("business owners") don't work.

**To identify archetypes, ask:**
- Who are the 5 most different types of people who need what I offer?
- What language does each group use to describe their problems?
- What are their specific wins and specific frustrations?

---

### Variable 5: Sub-Topic Expansion

Create variations of content by changing specific aspects within main themes.

Examples:
- Same topic, different audience segment (same tips, written for solopreneurs vs. enterprise)
- Same format, different timeframe ("in 30 days" vs. "in 6 months")
- Same insight, different industry example

## Content Matrix

The Content Bucket-Pillar Matrix intersects pillars with buckets to generate a grid of topic ideas.

**How to build it:**

1. Define 3-5 content pillars
2. For each pillar, generate one idea per bucket (Futurism, Your Story, Actionable Advice)
3. Result: a 3×3 to 5×3 matrix of topic ideas

**Output format:**

| Pillar | Futurism | Your Story | Actionable Advice |
|--------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Pillar 1 | [idea] | [idea] | [idea] |
| Pillar 2 | [idea] | [idea] | [idea] |
| Pillar 3 | [idea] | [idea] | [idea] |

Apply Magical Multipliers to expand any cell into 10 distinct posts.

## 10-Day Quick Start Framework

For users at Day Zero (no clients, no case studies yet):

Content must be built around:
- Personal story of why they chose this niche
- Planned process and methodology
- Observations from publicly available sources only
- Opinions and hypotheses

**10-day topic sequence:**
1. Origin story (why this niche)
2. Industry problem (what's broken)
3. Personal lesson (something they learned)
4. Industry misconception (what most people get wrong)
5. Personal failure (honest about the journey)
6. Controversial take on the industry
7. Industry opportunity (what's possible)

Each topic must include:
- A clear promise to the reader (X tips, Y steps, Z lessons)
- High-curiosity hook optimized for attention
- Format recommendation
- Honest acknowledgment of their current position (no fake case studies)

## Lean Content Multiplication Method

The fastest way to generate content ideas:

1. Start with your niche
2. Define 3-5 pillars
3. Map to 3 buckets → 9-15 topics
4. Apply 10 Magical Multipliers to each → 90-150 ideas
5. Filter by audience archetype → prioritize for specific segments

This produces months of content from a single planning session.
